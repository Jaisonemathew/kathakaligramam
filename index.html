About Ayirur Kathakali Gramam

Changing cities or villages names in India is trending and we often see the news that the name has been changed from this to that of that particular city or town. One such major change this time occurred in South India and this news is coming from the village famous for Kathakali dance form worldwide.

The Indian map will now represent Ayirur village in Kerala's Pathanamthitta as "Ayirur Kathakali Gramam" as a sign of the prominence given to the dance style Kathakali, famed for its lavishly colorful makeup and costumes.Kathakali performers from a small village on the banks of the Pampa River in this southern Kerala district have been performing tales from the Bible as well as Hindu Puranas for many years, earning them the right to associate their name with the ancient dance style.

Other attractions in Ayirur are

Velampady Aqueduct Bridge

Velampady Aqueduct Bridge is located in Ayroor South, Pathanamthitta.An aqueduct is a bridge that carries water. Navigable aqueducts, also called water bridges, allow vessels to cross valleys or ravines. Roman engineers developed aqueducts and viaducts, which are both bridge-like structures. Viaducts carry road or rail traffic over low ground or valleys.

Chathayam Vallamkali

Onam is also the season of snakeboat regatta in the Central Travancore district of Pathanamthitta The Ayroor grama panchayat will be organizing the Chathayam Vallamkali at Ayroor-Puthiyakavu, near Ranni, on September 6 afternoon. As many as 23 snakeboats belonging to the Palliyoda Karayogams on the Edakkulam-Edasserimala stretch of the Pampa river basin will take part in the boat pageantry and race

Hindumatha Parishath

"Hindumatha Parishath" which is held every year in the month of February since 1913 for propagating the essence of Hinduism in general and the teachings of PARAMA BHATTARAKA VIDYATHIRAJA CHATTAMBI SWAMIKAL, in particular. Swamiji was a great savant, thinker and philosopher of Kerala. He along with Sree Narayana Guru ushered in the great Rennaisance movement in Kerala 19th and early 20th centuary. The very fact that the Parishath continues to be held for the past 105 years, attracting and enlightening thousands of Hindu devotees, irrespective of caste and creed is itself proof of it wide and sustained acceptability.

PUTHEN SABARIMALA

Puthen Sabarimala, located in Thadiyoor, is a carbon copy of Sabarimala Temple, with similar rituals, rules, and opening and closing dates. The temple has a granite pathinettampadi, completed in 'Krishna Shila', which can only be ascended with 41 days vritham and irumudi. However, it differs in one aspect: ladies of all ages can enter through the northern gates, and devotees with lower vritham days can have Ayyappa darshan. Neiabhishekam is the most important offering.

Ayroor Padayani

Ayroor Puthiyakavu Bhagavathy Temple, located in Pathanamthitta, celebrates the annual Padayani festival on Revathy asterism in the Malayalam month of Kumbham (Mid March – Mid April). The padayani festival here is conducted as part of the Revathy Mahotsavam. Various Kolams performing at Ayroor Padayani are the Ganapathi, Kuthira, Pakshi, Yakshi, Marutha, Madan, Kaalan, Bhairavi and Mangala Bhairavi
